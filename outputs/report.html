Based on findings from Epoch AI's Frontier Data Centers Hub and X series.

Executive Summary

The landscape of AI infrastructure is undergoing a seismic shift. Epoch AI's research identifies a new class of "Gigawatt-Scale" data centers—facilities with power capacities exceeding 1000 MW (1 GW)—currently under construction. These projects, driven by hyperscalers and frontier model labs, represent a leap in physical scale and power consumption, comparable to entire metropolitan areas.

2. Hyperscalers & Labs: A Symbiotic Buildout

The construction is almost exclusively led by major hyperscalers (Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Oracle) acting as the infrastructure backbone for Frontier Model Labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI).

  • OpenAI is the primary driver for the two largest known projects (Fairwater and Stargate).
  • xAI is distinguishing itself with extreme speed, bypassing some traditional partnerships to build independently or with agile arrangements.
  • Anthropic leverages Amazon's heavy logistical and power infrastructure capabilities.

3. "Frontier Speed": Compressing Timeline

Despite the massive increase in scale (from typical ~100 MW sites to >1000 MW), build times are compressing.

  • xAI's Colossus 2 targets a 12-month completion, a pace previously thought impossible for this scale.
  • Typical timelines operate around 2 years, significantly faster than traditional utility-scale infrastructure projects.

Projections for Future Expansion

Based on the current trajectory and the data from Epoch AI, we project the following trends for 2026-2030:

1. The "5 GW Campus" by 2030

With 3.3 GW already planned for 2027, we project that 5 GW campuses will enter planning stages by late 2025. These will likely require dedicated SMR (Small Modular Reactor) or direct grid-interconnect agreements that bypass traditional utility queues.

2. Geographic Centralization

Construction is clustering in regions with specific characteristics:

  • High Power Availability: Areas near major transmission lines or power plants (e.g., nuclear sites).
  • Land Availability: Rural areas in Texas, the Midwest (Indiana, Wisconsin), and the South (Louisiana, Georgia).
  • Regulatory Speed: States with streamlined permitting processes will capture the bulk of this investment.

3. Power as the Primary Bottleneck

The constraint on expansion will shift entirely from silicon availability (GPUs) to power availability. Future expansion will be dictated not by capital but by the ability to secure gigawatt-scale interconnects. We expect hyperscalers to begin vertically integrating power generation (investing directly in solar + storage or nuclear) to guarantee supply for these 1 GW+ sites.
